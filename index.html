Progressive Early Mobility Algorithm

A Facilitator Guide for Early Mobility Protocols

Early mobility of patients admitted to the medical intensive care unit is crucial to optimal patient outcomes.

An early mobility algorithm integrated into patient care can enhance mobilization of the critical-ill as one transition through their course of hospitalization.

Using a tool to help screen for the appropriateness of early mobility helps determine whether a patient is clinically ready to attempt early mobilization. This algorithm begins by reviewing the following question:

Does patient respond adequately to verbal stimulation? Richmond Agitation Sedation Scale (RASS) > -3

Initiate early mobility assessment.

Does the patient at least meet the following exclusion criteria:

Respiratory criteria

  • Fraction of Inspired Oxygen (FiO2) > 0.6
  • Positive End-Expiratory Pressure (PEEP) > 10cm H2O
  • Hypoxemia: Pulse oximetry < 88 %
  • Tachypnea: Respiratory Rate > 35
  • Acidosis: Arterial pH Rate < 7.25

Circulatory criteria

  • New or increased vasopressor dose within past 2h
  • Continous infusion of a vasodilator medication
  • Addition of a new anti-arrythmia agent within past 24h
  • Unstable arrythmia within 24h
  • Mean arterial pressure (MAP) >140 mmHg OR < 55 mmHg
  • New Deep Vein Thrombosis/ Pulmonary Embolism (first 24h)

Neurologic criteria

  • Acute stroke (first 24h)

Other exclusion considerations

  • Hemoglobin > 7gm
  • Positive End-Expiratory Pressure (PEEP) > 10cm H2O
  • PLatelet count < 20,000
  • Temporary transvenous pacemaker
  • Femoral arterial OR venous line
  • Intra-Aortic Balloon Pump

Is lack of response rooted in either Central Nervous System (CNS) etiology or unstable Intracranial Pressure (ICP)?

Initiate Level 1 mobility protocol.

Early Mobility Program should focus on:

  • Passive ROM
  • Passive Bed Cycling

Begin Level 2 mobility protocol.

Early Mobility Program should focus on:

  • PT consultation
  • Sitting on edge of bed
  • Resistance training
  • Passive or active leg and/or arm cycling in bed or chair
  • Active ROM exercises

Can patient sit unassisted? Guidelines note that if patients can tolerate chair activity, they should always sit upright in a chair at mealtimes.

Ask physician to change sedation measures if possible so the patient is only mildly sedated at most. Caregivers should then perform DATs every 12 hours.

Reassess in 24 hours.

Advance to Level 3 mobility protocol.

Early Mobility Program should focus on:

  • Sit-to-stand and static standing at bedside
  • Moving legs against gravity
  • Pivot transfer bed to chair
  • ADL training
  • Active leg and/or arm cycling in bed or chair

Can patient stand with assistance?

Confine physiotherapy to Level 2 mobility protocol.

Early Mobility Program should focus on:

  • PT consultation
  • Sitting on edge of bed
  • Resistance training
  • Passive or active leg and/or arm cycling in bed or chair
  • Active ROM exercises

Advance to Level 4 mobility protocol.

Early Mobility Program should focus on:

  • Ambulation
  • Strength building
  • Gait training

Confine physiotherapy to Level 3 mobility protocol.

Early Mobility Program should focus on:

  • Sit-to-stand and static standing at bedside
  • Moving legs against gravity
  • Pivot transfer bed to chair
  • ADL training
  • Active leg and/or arm cycling in bed or chair
